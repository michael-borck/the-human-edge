# Frameworks overview

## The tools you'll keep using Monday

These are durable, tool-agnostic frameworks: they work regardless of which AI model you're using or which one comes out next quarter. They span both halves of the day — the task scale (morning) and the project scale (afternoon).

## The trust tool — the spine of the day

**Average / Precise × Small / Large.** Two questions turn "where does a human stay in the loop?" from a gut feel into a decision you can defend. Learned in the morning at the task level, reused in the afternoon at the project level.

## RTCF — directing AI well

**R**ole · **T**ask · **C**ontext · **F**ormat. Context is where *your* edge enters: the specifics only you hold.

## The five differences — why AI delivery breaks the rules

"Done" can't be specified · the demo is a trap · the data is the uncertainty · verification is the product · generic competence is the baseline.

## Scale / Pivot / Kill — the go/no-go call

At each gate, the honest question isn't "is it perfect?" It's whether the evidence justifies continuing. Killing early is success.
