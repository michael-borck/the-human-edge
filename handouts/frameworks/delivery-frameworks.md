# Delivery frameworks: readiness, assumptions & the go/no-go

Three quick frameworks for the afternoon's project – **classify the ambition**, **check you're ready**, then **make a go/no-go you can defend**. Drawn from ISYS6020 (*AI in Business: Strategy and Management*) and *Conversation, Not Delegation*.

## 1. The AI Transformation Matrix – how ambitious is this?

Classify the initiative *before* you plan. The quadrant sets the bar for readiness, risk and effort.

| | **Process focus** | **Strategic focus** |
|---|---|---|
| **Incremental value** | **OPTIMISE** – do existing things cheaper (e.g. automated data entry) | **ENHANCE** – better decisions, sharper competitive position (e.g. customer insights) |
| **Transformational value** | **REVOLUTIONISE** – reinvent how the work gets done (e.g. predictive maintenance) | **TRANSFORM** – new business model, AI-native offering |

The further toward *Transform*, the higher the stakes – and the more readiness and human-in-the-loop you need.

## 2. The AI readiness check – are we ready?

Three lenses, run before you scope:

- **Data readiness** – do we have the data, and is it good enough? (You discover this by working with it, not by asking.) Who owns it? What's missing or messy?
- **Organisational readiness** – who owns this initiative, who's affected, who'll resist? Is the sponsor still bought in?
- **Build vs buy vs host** – how will we deliver it? Buy a vendor product, build it ourselves, or self-host? Each carries different cost, vendor lock-in and governance consequences.

Low readiness in any lens doesn't kill the project – it **reshapes the scope**: start smaller, fix the data first, buy before you build.

## 3. The assumptions register – making the go/no-go defensible

Every plan rests on assumptions. Name them, and the Scale / Pivot / Kill call stops being a gut feel.

List the 3–5 **load-bearing assumptions** your plan depends on, and how sensitive the plan is to each:

| Assumption (what must be true) | Sensitivity (if it's wrong, the plan…) | Status at the gate |
|---|---|---|
| *"the returns-policy docs are current and complete"* | …fails on policy accuracy | holding / shaky / broken |
| *"the bot will route to a human when unsure"* | …erodes trust fast | holding / shaky / broken |
| *"the CS team has capacity to review"* | …checkpoints become a bottleneck | holding / shaky / broken |

Then the call writes itself:

- **Scale** – the load-bearing assumptions are **holding**.
- **Pivot** – one key assumption is **shaky but fixable** (change the shape, keep the intent).
- **Kill** – a core assumption has **broken**, and the cost of continuing outweighs the cost of stopping.

Killing early is success, not failure – the assumptions register is what lets you do it **with evidence**.
