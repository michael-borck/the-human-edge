# Make your edge persistent: the two-page voice method

The morning's two-pass demo showed the whole thesis in five minutes: ask AI naively and you get the same slick, generic answer everyone else gets. Add what only *you* hold — your context, your taste, your angle — and the output gets a soul. That's the edge, injected one prompt at a time.

This page is how you make that edge **permanent**, so you don't have to re-supply it on every request. The technique comes from AI researcher Ethan Mollick; the tool below is free and runs in your browser.

## The method, in three steps

1. **Give it a sample of your writing.** A few hundred words of something you actually wrote — an email, a report, a blog post. The more it sounds like *you*, the better.
2. **Let it write a style brief.** Ask the AI to analyse the sample and produce a one-to-two-page description of your style, *including instructions for how to write in it*. (This is the key move: you want a description of your voice, not more of your writing.)
3. **Paste that brief into your AI's custom instructions.** Tell it "write in this style" for future tasks. From then on, the generic average tilts toward *your* average.

## The tool: Style Mirror

[**Style Mirror**](https://stylemirror.eduserver.au) (stylemirror.eduserver.au) does steps 1 and 2 for you, in the browser:

- Open the **Analyze** tab, paste your writing, hit *Extract Style DNA*.
- It returns an editable **Style DNA** profile — tone, structure, vocabulary, quirks — which is your style brief.
- Copy that profile into your AI platform's custom instructions.

It's provider-agnostic. **It runs on a local model (Ollama)**, which means your writing sample never leaves your machine — worth choosing if the sample is work-sensitive. (That's the data-responsibility call this course keeps returning to.)

## The honest part — and why it's still on-thesis

Mollick is blunt that the result is a **"slight parody" of you**, not a perfect clone. Don't expect it to carry a long-form piece like a book. That caveat is the interesting bit, because it's the thesis landing a second time:

> Even *personalised*, the AI is still producing an **average of you** — a smoothed, stylised version of your voice. The part it can't capture is your live judgement: the taste call you make in the moment, the thing you'd say here but not there.

So the goal isn't to outsource your voice. It's to clear the generic baseline out of the way — the part anyone could have written — so the bit that's left, the part only you would write, is easier to spot and protect.

That's also why mimicking *yourself* beats asking the AI to mimic a famous writer. Hemingway's style is already in the training data — it's the ultimate generic average. Your voice is the one thing only you can supply.

## What you leave with

A two-page style brief, pasted into your AI's custom instructions, that quietly turns "generic" into "yours" — and a clearer sense of where the edge still lives after that: with you.
