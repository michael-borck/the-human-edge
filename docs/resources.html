Free AI tools to explore

TipHands-on, no account needed (mostly)
  • Our class NotebookLM — the notebook we built together in the session. Add your own sources and ask it anything, source-grounded. (The clearest way to feel the “convincing average” at scale.)
  • Google NotebookLM — start your own: paste a source (webpage, PDF, your notes) and get a podcast, a briefing doc, a study guide. Free and source-grounded.
  • Google Teachable Machine — train your own image or audio classifier in two minutes, in the browser. AI beyond text — feel how pattern-matching works.
  • Survival of the Best Fit — play ~10 minutes and watch an “objective” hiring algorithm amplify the bias in its training data.
  • Style Mirror — give it a sample of your writing; it extracts a two-page style brief to paste into your custom instructions. (The “make your edge persistent” method.)
  • CiteSight — verify references, check citation formatting, and catch the suspicious ones. Free online and desktop. Exactly the defence against AI’s most common failure: a polished reference that doesn’t exist.
